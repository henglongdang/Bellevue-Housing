Data Lab

From Civic Investigation to Data Science Portfolio

The Data Lab describes how the project can grow from manual research into structured datasets, statistical modeling, predictive analysis, and policy simulation.

Dataset Inventory

Inputs for Future Analysis

Housing prices

Sale prices, list prices, price reductions, days on market, and geographic identifiers.

Mortgage rates

Weekly rate series and buyer payment simulations for affordability stress testing.

REET schedules

State graduated brackets, local rates, and historical changes by jurisdiction.

Local tax rates

Comparable municipal tax structures and boundary-based policy differences.

Interview coding results

Qualitative themes transformed into structured codes such as seller sensitivity and rate concern.

Open house observations

Visitor categories, repeated questions, and field notes about buyer priorities.

Policy documents

Statutes, budget references, and capital-plan passages tagged by revenue purpose.

Future Analysis Pipeline

Five Phases of Expansion

Phase 1

Manual analysis

Continue hand-coded document review and interview synthesis.

Phase 2

Structured database

Create tables for transactions, rates, observations, and document metadata.

Phase 3

Statistical modeling

Estimate associations between tax burdens, listing behavior, and liquidity indicators.

Phase 4

Predictive housing models

Model probability of price cuts, days-on-market changes, and transaction slowdown.

Phase 5

Policy simulation engine

Test tiered, flexible, or replacement-revenue designs under alternative market conditions.

Machine Learning Expansion

Future Possibilities

Housing liquidity prediction

Forecast when listings may stall under combined rate and tax pressure.

Market friction modeling

Measure how cumulative closing costs influence seller and buyer behavior.

Municipal tax simulations

Compare revenue stability under flat, tiered, temporary, or cyclical REET structures.

Policy impact forecasting

Estimate buyer affordability and capital-budget effects before policy adoption.

Geographic affordability mapping

Map boundary effects and affordability pressure across Eastside neighborhoods.
